Vitalik Buterin

  • 1994 – Born on January 31 in Kolomna, Russia, to Dmitry Buterin (a computer scientist) and Natalia Ameline. His family soon recognized his unusual intellectual abilities.
  • 2000 – Moves with his parents to Canada at age 6. In school, teachers notice his talent for mathematics and programming. He is placed into a gifted program where he develops an early interest in economics and computer science.
  • 2011 – At 17, Vitalik co-founds Bitcoin Magazine, one of the first serious publications dedicated to cryptocurrency. He writes hundreds of articles explaining Bitcoin's mechanics and potential, becoming a respected voice in the emerging crypto community.
  • 2013 – Publishes the Ethereum white paper, outlining a vision for a blockchain platform that could run smart contracts—self-executing agreements coded directly into the blockchain. This idea expands blockchain beyond currency into a platform for decentralized applications.
  • 2014 – Drops out of the University of Waterloo after receiving the Thiel Fellowship, a $100,000 grant from PayPal co-founder Peter Thiel to pursue Ethereum full-time.
  • 2015 – Ethereum officially launches. Developers around the world begin building decentralized applications (dApps) on the platform. Ethereum quickly becomes the second-largest cryptocurrency by market capitalization.
  • 2016 – The infamous DAO hack occurs, draining millions of dollars from a decentralized investment fund built on Ethereum. Vitalik helps lead the community through the crisis, which results in a controversial hard fork that splits Ethereum into two chains: Ethereum (ETH) and Ethereum Classic (ETC).
  • 2017 – Ethereum becomes the backbone of the ICO boom, where startups raise billions by issuing tokens. Vitalik warns of scams and unsustainable hype, showing his cautious approach to crypto's explosive growth.
  • 2018–2019 – Advocates for scaling solutions to Ethereum's congestion problems. He promotes sharding and layer-2 rollups as ways to increase transaction throughput.
  • 2020 – Plays a central role in guiding Ethereum's transition to Ethereum 2.0, introducing proof-of-stake consensus to reduce energy consumption compared to proof-of-work mining.
  • 2022 – Ethereum completes The Merge, one of the most significant upgrades in blockchain history. The network successfully transitions from proof-of-work to proof-of-stake, cutting energy usage by over 99%. Vitalik calls it “the beginning of a new era for Ethereum.”
  • 2023–2025 – Continues to advocate for decentralized governance, privacy-preserving technologies, and scaling solutions. He emphasizes the importance of building systems that are resistant to censorship and centralized control.
  • 2025 – Recognized globally as one of the most influential figures in blockchain, living in Zug, Switzerland, and still actively shaping Ethereum's future.
